---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ports are physical interfaces on a computer or electronic device that allow for the connection of peripherals and cables. 
They facilitate the transfer of data, power, or signals between devices. 

Ports are the receptacles on a device (such as a laptop, desktop, or monitor) into which connectors are inserted. 
They provide the interface through which data, power, or signals are transmitted.

Example: The USB socket on a laptop is a port. The HDMI socket on a monitor is a port.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Here are some common types of ports:
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Common Types of Ports
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
USB Ports (Universal Serial Bus)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
USB Type-A		: The most common and widely used USB port. Used for connecting devices like keyboards, mice, flash drives, and printers.

USB Type-C		: A newer, smaller, and reversible port used for data transfer, power delivery, and video output. Found on many modern laptops, tablets, and smartphones.

Micro USB and Mini USB	: Smaller USB ports used mainly on older mobile devices and cameras.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HDMI Ports (High-Definition Multimedia Interface)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Used for transmitting high-definition video and audio from a computer to a monitor, TV, or projector.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DisplayPort
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Used for transmitting digital video and audio signals, similar to HDMI, but often preferred for computer monitors.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ethernet Port (RJ-45)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Used for wired network connections, allowing a computer to connect to the internet or a local network.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Audio Ports
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.5mm Audio Jack		: Commonly used for headphones, speakers, and microphones.

Optical Audio Port (TOSLINK)	: Used for transmitting digital audio signals.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
VGA Port (Video Graphics Array)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
An older analog port used for connecting monitors. Still found on some older devices and projectors.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DVI Port (Digital Visual Interface)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Used for connecting monitors. Can transmit both analog and digital signals.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Thunderbolt Port
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
High-speed port used for data transfer, video output, and power delivery. 
Commonly found on Apple devices and high-end PCs. Thunderbolt 3 and 4 use the USB Type-C connector.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SD Card Slot
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Used for reading memory cards, typically from cameras and other portable devices.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Specialized Ports
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
eSATA (External Serial Advanced Technology Attachment) : Used for connecting external storage devices.

FireWire (IEEE 1394)				       : Used for high-speed data transfer, often in video editing and professional audio 
applications. Less common today.

PS/2 Port					       : An older port used for connecting keyboards and mice.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ports are essential for connecting various peripherals and ensuring seamless communication and power supply between the computer and 
connected devices.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Difference Between Port and Connectors.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Connectors plug into ports. They are part of cables or peripheral devices.
Ports are built into devices.
