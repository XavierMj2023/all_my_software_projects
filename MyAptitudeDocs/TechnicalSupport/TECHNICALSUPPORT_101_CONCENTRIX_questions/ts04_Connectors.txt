Connectors
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Connectors are interfaces that allow different computer components and peripherals to communicate with each other or to the motherboard. 
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Here are some common types:
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Power Connectors:
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
24-pin  ATX Connector				: 	Powers the motherboard.
4/8-pin CPU Connector				: 	Provides power to the CPU.
SATA Power Connector				: 	Supplies power to storage devices like HDDs and SSDs.
PCIe Power Connector				: 	Powers high-performance components like graphics cards.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Data Connectors:
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SATA (Serial ATA)				: 	Connects storage devices like HDDs, SSDs, and optical drives to the motherboard.
M.2						: 	Connects high-speed storage devices directly to the motherboard.
IDE (Integrated Drive Electronics)		: 	An older standard for connecting storage devices.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Peripheral Connectors:
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
USB (Universal Serial Bus)			: 	Connects various peripherals like keyboards, mice, printers, and external storage devices.
HDMI (High-Definition Multimedia Interface)	: 	Transmits audio and video signals to monitors and TVs.
DisplayPort					: 	Similar to HDMI, used for connecting monitors.
VGA (Video Graphics Array)			: 	An older standard for video output to monitors.
DVI (Digital Visual Interface)			: 	Used for video output to monitors.
Audio Jacks					: 	For connecting speakers, headphones, and microphones.
Ethernet (RJ-45)				: 	For wired network connections.
PS/2						: 	Older connectors for keyboards and mice.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Internal Connectors:
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PCIe (Peripheral Component Interconnect Express): Slots for adding expansion cards like GPUs, sound cards, and network cards.
RAM Slots (DIMM Slots)				: For installing RAM modules.
Fan Connectors					: For connecting case and CPU fans.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Connectors are physical interfaces or ports that allow different electronic components or devices to communicate with each other 
by transmitting data, power, or signals. 

They serve as the link between the internal components of a computer, such as the motherboard, power supply, and storage devices
and external peripherals like keyboards, mice, and monitors.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Here are some simplified explanations of common connectors:
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Power Connectors
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
24-pin ATX Connector		: 	This large connector from the power supply unit (PSU) provides the main power to the motherboard.
4/8-pin CPU Connector		: 	Supplies additional power to the CPU, ensuring it has enough power for intensive tasks.
SATA Power Connector		: 	Delivers power from the PSU to storage devices like hard drives (HDDs) and solid-state drives (SSDs).

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Data Connectors
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SATA Connector			: A thin, flat cable that connects storage devices (like HDDs and SSDs) to the motherboard, allowing data transfer between them.

M.2 Connector			: A slot on the motherboard for connecting high-speed storage devices, like NVMe SSDs, directly.

USB Connector			: A versatile port used for connecting a wide range of devices, such as keyboards, mice, flash drives, and external hard drives.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Peripheral Connectors
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
USB Ports (Universal Serial Bus)			: Found on the computer case, these ports connect external devices like mice, keyboards, and external drives.

HDMI Port (High-Definition Multimedia Interface)	: Transmits high-definition video and audio from the computer to a monitor or TV.

Ethernet Port (RJ-45)					: Used for wired network connections, allowing the computer to connect to the internet or a local network.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Internal Connectors
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PCIe Slots (Peripheral Component Interconnect Express)	: Slots on the motherboard where expansion cards (like graphics cards, sound cards, or network cards) are installed.

RAM Slots (DIMM Slots)					: Slots on the motherboard for installing RAM modules, which are crucial for the computerâ€™s temporary data storage and quick access.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Examples to Illustrate
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
USB Connector: When you plug a USB flash drive into a USB port on your computer, the connector enables data transfer between the flash drive and the computer.

HDMI Connector: When you connect your computer to a monitor using an HDMI cable, the HDMI connectors on both devices transmit video and audio signals.
Simplified Visualization

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Imagine connectors as the plug-and-play parts of your computer.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
USB Ports		: Like the outlets in your home where you plug in various appliances.
HDMI Ports		: Like the socket for your TV cable, delivering both picture and sound.
Power Connectors	: Like the power sockets that provide electricity to your devices.

The connector for a mouse to a laptop can be one of the following types:
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
USB Connector
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
USB Type-A	: The most common connector type for wired mice. It is a rectangular port found on most laptops and desktops.

USB Type-C	: A newer, smaller, and reversible connector that is increasingly found on modern laptops. Some newer mice use this connector type.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Wireless Connectors
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
USB Wireless Receiver (Dongle)	: Many wireless mice come with a small USB dongle that you plug into a USB port on your laptop. The mouse communicates with the dongle via radio frequency (RF).

Bluetooth			: Some mice connect directly to the laptop via Bluetooth, eliminating the need for a USB dongle. The laptop must have built-in Bluetooth capability, or you can use a Bluetooth adapter.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Older Connectors
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PS/2 Connector			: An older round connector used for mice and keyboards on older computers. It is rarely found on modern laptops.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
For most modern laptops, the USB Type-A and Bluetooth connections are the most common and convenient ways to connect a mouse.
