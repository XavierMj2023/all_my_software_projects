A desktop computer typically consists of several internal components that work together to perform computing tasks. 


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Here are the key internal components:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Motherboard:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The central printed circuit board (PCB) that houses the CPU, RAM, and other essential components.
Provides connectors for other peripherals.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Central Processing Unit (CPU):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The primary processor that performs calculations and executes instructions.
Often referred to as the brain of the computer.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Random Access Memory (RAM):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Volatile memory used to store data and instructions temporarily while the computer is on.
Allows for quick access to data by the CPU.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Power Supply Unit (PSU):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Converts electrical power from an outlet into a usable form for the computer.
Supplies power to all internal components.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Storage Devices:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Hard Disk Drive (HDD) or Solid State Drive (SSD):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HDDs use spinning disks to read/write data magnetically.
SSDs use flash memory for faster data access and retrieval.
Store the operating system, applications, and data.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Graphics Processing Unit (GPU):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Handles rendering of images, video, and animations.
Can be integrated into the CPU or installed as a separate, dedicated graphics card.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cooling System:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CPU Cooler: Can be an air cooler or a liquid cooler to dissipate heat from the CPU.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Case Fans: Help circulate air through the case to maintain optimal temperature.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Optical Drive (optional):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Used to read/write CDs, DVDs, or Blu-ray discs.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Network Interface Card (NIC):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Provides connectivity to a network, either wired (Ethernet) or wireless (Wi-Fi).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expansion Slots:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PCI Express (PCIe) slots for additional cards such as extra GPUs, sound cards, or network cards.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BIOS/UEFI Firmware:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Firmware interface that initializes hardware during the boot process and provides runtime services for the operating system.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cables and Connectors:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Power Cables: Connect the PSU to various components.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Data Cables: Such as SATA cables for connecting storage devices to the motherboard.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
